// Research Areas

What We Study

Our group investigates quantum and nanoscale systems using theoretical and computational methods, spanning mesoscopic transport, topological phases, non-Hermitian physics, and nanomechanics.

01
Mesoscopic Quantum Transport
Electron transport in mesoscopic systems including topological insulators, graphene, 2D materials, Chern insulators, and superconducting nanostructures.
02
Non-Hermitian Topology
Topological phases in non-Hermitian systems — skin effects, exceptional points, Hopf bundle matter, and bulk-boundary correspondence beyond the Hermitian paradigm.
03
Photonic & Wave Chaos
Wave chaos in optical microcavities and photonic crystals, Bloch-theorem-dictated quantum scar modes, and moiré photonic superlattices.
04
Flat Bands & Localization
Compact localized states, symmetry-protected flat bands, dynamical localization in driven quantum systems, and topological flat band formation in moiré systems.
05
Nano-Electromechanical Systems
Quantum phenomena in nanomechanical resonators, Cooper pair box qubits, diamond nanomechanical mass spectrometry, and quasiparticle dynamics in superconducting qubits.
06
Graphene & 2D Valleytronics
Valley isospin manipulation in strained graphene, graphene qubits via strain engineering, and quantum transport in graphene p-n junctions and Chern insulator junctions.
